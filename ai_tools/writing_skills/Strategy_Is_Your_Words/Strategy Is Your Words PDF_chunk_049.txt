Summary of Chapter 38: Who Gets to Have Ideas?
Understanding "As a Creative"
The phrase “as a creative” often reflects an attempt to
establish hierarchical boundaries in the realm of creativity,
suggesting that the speaker is unique or exceptional while
implying that listeners are not. This notion can shortcut
deeper discussions about creativity in work environments.
The Network of Creativity
Several interconnected beliefs about creativity are
highlighted:
- Creativity involves the act of generating ideas from diverse
elements.
- It represents an interplay between uncertainty and certainty.
- Creativity is inherent in all humans, and its destruction can

[PÁGINA 157]

also be a form of creativity.
- Acceptance by peers can lead to new norms based on ideas.
- Mastery of creativity requires technique and practice, but
also varies among individuals.
Critique of Creativity Claims
Efforts to monopolize the concept of creativity ultimately
limit others rather than empower them. Phrases like "ideas
can come from anywhere" can be misleading; while they
promote inclusivity, they can also justify exploiting talent
without recognizing the value of expertise.
The Myth of Universal Creativity
While the idea that “everyone is creative” promotes hope, it
doesn't equate everyone to be exceptionally creative in
practice. This belief can dilute the understanding of creativity
and diminish the recognition of individual achievements in
the field.
