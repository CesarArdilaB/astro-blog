How Insights Happen
Nature of Insights
Insights can arise in various forms, often triggered by
statements or observations that evoke unexpected
connections or emotions. For example, a phrase like “I
haven’t achieved enough to go bald” can serve as a
springboard for deeper understanding, highlighting the
intersection of self-image and achievement.
Insight Generation Process
Insights emerge not only from casual conversation but can
also stem from academic measurements that link behaviors,
such as couples discussing donation amounts leading to
actual donations. This process involves critical questioning to

Questions That Fetch Insights
Questions are essential for gaining insights, yet opinions on
their value vary among strategists. Some might avoid asking
because they equate questions with weakness, while others
may not prioritize formulating questions before beginning
research. A few strategists adhere strictly to academic
research methods, which may be admirable but can be
intimidating for newcomers.
It’s critical to ask both serious and loaded questions during
interviews to uncover deeper feelings and thoughts from
respondents. Questions like "What makes you angry that
others don’t know about?" or "What secrets do you keep
about this?" can provoke meaningful responses. Establishing
a nonjudgmental rapport with interviewees is vital, as many
people rarely have someone who listens to them, which can
prompt them to share insights.
