Summary of the Strategy Brain Process
A well-executed strategy should pass through several
checkpoints:
1. Confront the identified problem.
2. Incorporate a central idea.
3. Connect to a significant theme.
4. Be defensible in its approach.
5. Be visualizable in a compelling way.

Ten Ways to Write a Strategy
As strategies evolve into plans, they can become cluttered
with unnecessary details. The essential question to maintain
clarity and effectiveness is: “What’s the smallest unit of
communication I can use to express this strategy?” Here are
ten techniques:
1. Strategy Statements
Use direct phrases like “Show that X is Y” to clearly
communicate the strategy, keeping the audience in mind.
*Example:* Show that the New York Knicks are the best
anger management in town.
2. Single-Minded Propositions
Identify the most crucial message that needs to be
communicated. Blend these with the unique selling

[PÁGINA 244]

proposition to create impactful statements.
*Example:* The New York Knicks cure New York anger.
3. Brand Essences
Brand essences encapsulate strategic ideas but can become
vague and cliché. Aim for clarity over confusion.
*Example:* New York Anger Management.
4. CEO Announcements
Watch for shifts in strategy through CEO statements that
simplify complex ideas into broader concepts.
*Example:* “We aren’t in the business of basketball, we’re
in the business of anger management.”
5. Purpose Statements
A clear purpose explains how a brand serves society. It
should be straightforward and deeply integrated into the
business model.
