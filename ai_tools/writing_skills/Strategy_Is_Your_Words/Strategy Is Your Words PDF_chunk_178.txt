8.Question
In what ways should a strategy confront the problem at
its core?
Answer:A strategy should confront the problem by clearly
identifying it, presenting a unique and compelling insight
about it, and connecting to broader themes that resonate in
the business. This confrontation will lay the foundation for
meaningful solutions and strategic initiatives.

1.Question
What is the essence of a strategy statement and how can it
be effectively crafted?
Answer:A strategy statement should encapsulate the
core idea of the strategy in a concise way, using a
structure like 'Show that X is Y'. It works best when
it includes an imperative verb, indicating direct
action intended for an internal audience. For

[PÁGINA 619]

example, 'Show that the New York Knicks cure New
York anger' conveys a strong message simply and
clearly.
2.Question
How does a single-minded proposition differ from
strategy statements, and what is its importance?
Answer:A single-minded proposition narrows down the core
message to what is most critical to communicate. It answers
the question 'What’s the most important thing we need to
communicate?' This focus is vital as it helps to clarify the
unique selling proposition and guides strategic decisions,
keeping the message effective and engaging.
3.Question
Why should strategy writing avoid adjective-heavy
language?
Answer:Adjective-heavy language can lead to confusion and
dilution of meaning. Instead, focusing on strong nouns and
direct verbs helps maintain clarity and impact. Adjectives can
become vague and corporate-speak, while strong action

[PÁGINA 620]

words clearly delineate ideas and actions, making them easier
for others to grasp.
4.Question
What makes a great brand essence statement, and what
should be avoided?
Answer:A great brand essence statement should be clear,
meaningful, and anchored in genuine value without resorting
to pretentious or vague language. Avoid trying to sound
'corporate' with overly complicated or cringeworthy phrases
like 'nutritiously delicious'. Instead, aim for straightforward
expressions that resonate easily with the audience, such as
'New York Anger Management'.
