necessity of empathy in decision-making?

Chapter 60 | Path to Purchase| Q&A
1.Question
What is the Path to Purchase and how can it be applied?
Answer:The Path to Purchase is a framework that
illustrates how consumers transition from being
unaware of a product to making a purchase. It can
be applied generally to understand broad consumer
behavior, or it can focus on a specific audience, such
as aquarium owners who love basketball, to pinpoint
their unique buying habits. By differentiating
between mapping existing behaviors and planning
strategic marketing actions, businesses can better
align their efforts based on consumer insights.
2.Question
What are the key questions used in the Path to Purchase
as a map?
Answer:Key questions as a map include: 1) Awareness: How

[PÁGINA 597]

do people learn about the product? 2) Consideration: What
prompts them to consider the product? 3) Intent: What
factors lead them to add it to their shopping list? 4) Trial:
How do they try the product? 5) Purchase: What steps do
they take to buy it? 6) Usage: How do they use the product?
7) Repurchase: What encourages them to buy it again?
3.Question
How does the Path to Purchase change when used as a
plan?
Answer:When used as a plan, the Path to Purchase
transforms the questions to focus on strategic actions: 1)
Awareness: How will we make people aware of our product?
2) Consideration: How will we get people to add it to their
shopping list? 3) Intent: How will we motivate intention to
purchase? 4) Trial: How will we encourage trial use? 5)
Purchase: How will we facilitate sales? 6) Usage: How will
we ensure effective usage? 7) Repurchase: How will we
stimulate repeat purchases?
