Writing Insights

[PÁGINA 204]

You have the freedom to articulate insights in your own way,
as well as to define what “insight” means to you. Effective
writing techniques can enhance insights by introducing
twists, turning points, or surprises. Framing insights as
surprises helps highlight the contrast between what is
commonly known and what is revealed.
The concept of a Surprise! is vital—it allows for a leap from
one understood idea to another, creating a new context.
Unlike a three-legged race that hampers movement, a
well-crafted insight propels understanding forward, helping
to connect disparate ideas in innovative ways.

How to Help a Workshop Write an Insight
The Challenge of Public Insight Creation
In many businesses, there is pressure to demonstrate
productivity, leading teams to seek insights in public
settings. This approach is difficult because genuine insights
often require private reflection and the possibility of
exploration before clarity emerges.
Techniques for Coaxing Ideas
To facilitate idea generation in a group setting, several
conversational techniques can be employed. These
techniques help participants break free from their business
personas and express raw thoughts. Examples include:
- "You know how...?"
- "You know what’s funny?"

[PÁGINA 206]

- "The thing is..."
- "It’s like..."
- "People believe X, but Y is true."
- "Say it like a comedian."
These phrases encourage attendees to share their insights and
heighten engagement, using relatable examples and humor.
Framing Insights Through Templates
Offering a template can guide participants in structuring their
insights. For instance, using statements like "People believe
X, but I know Y" helps distill complex thoughts into clearer
messages.
Shifting Perspectives
Encouraging participants to express ideas humorously or in
simplified terms can shift their mindset. For example,
starting with comedic gibberish can help unlock creative
thinking and generate punchy ideas.
