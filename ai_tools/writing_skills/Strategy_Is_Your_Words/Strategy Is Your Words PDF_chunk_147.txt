Chapter 42 | These Ideas Are Scary, Aren’t They?|
Q&A
1.Question
What do the ideas presented in this chapter invoke in us,
according to the author?
Answer:They invoke a mix of thrill and fear,
suggesting that although we might be excited about
the ideas, the responsibility that comes with them
can be intimidating.
2.Question
Why does the author frame the acceptance of ideas as
similar to taking in unexpected 'human lumps'?
Answer:This analogy emphasizes that accepting ideas is a
significant responsibility, akin to caring for unexpected
children. It requires commitment and effort to nurture and

[PÁGINA 520]

develop those ideas into something meaningful.
3.Question
What are the three choices presented when confronting
new ideas?
Answer:The three choices are to avoid the ideas, reject the
ideas, or accept the ideas, each with different consequences
for personal growth and development.
4.Question
What metaphor does the author use to describe the option
of mindless running in life?
Answer:The author metaphorically suggests that mindless
running leads to forgetting one's purpose or direction, leaving
one exhausted and without answers, which is far scarier than
facing fear.
5.Question
How can fear be perceived when choosing a life of play
according to the chapter?
Answer:In a life of play, fear can be viewed as merely a toy,
something to be engaged with joyfully rather than something
to avoid or dread.
