Chapter 58 Summary : The Product
World
The Product World Overview
The Product World is an exploratory approach to
understanding products or services in their natural settings
without imposing a rigid structure. It aims to capture raw
insights from users about their experiences, thoughts, and
behaviors.
Key Elements to Capture
1.
Features:
Discussed product characteristics.
2.
Functions:
Ways users utilize those features.
3.
Benefits:
Prominent advantages highlighted by users.

[PÁGINA 223]

4.
Triggers:
Factors prompting searches or purchases.
5.
Use Cases:
Typical applications of the product/service.
6.
Edge Cases:
Uncommon or unique usages.
Methodology
- Maintain natural language, avoiding business jargon.
- Emphasize user perspectives to grasp product perceptions
and decision-making processes.
- Start broadly and then narrow down to specific groups for
deeper insights.
Example Case: New York Advertising Agencies
-
Features:
Demographic and professional specifics about potential
hires.

-
Triggers:
Contextual events that necessitate hiring.
-
Use Cases:
Various scenarios in which the product/service is utilized.
-
Edge Cases:
Unconventional actions or uses related to the roles.
Understanding Inputs
