Chapter 52 | Questions That Fetch Insights| Q&A
1.Question
Why is it important to ask questions during research?
Answer:Asking questions is a fundamental way to
gain insights. Questions guide your research by
focusing your efforts on the right topics, helping you
uncover deeper truths that mere data collection
might not reveal. Instead of just gathering
information, questions allow you to explore the
inner thoughts and feelings of respondents.
2.Question
What types of questions can elicit deeper insights?
Answer:To find richer insights, consider asking loaded and
personal questions such as: "What’s something nobody else
knows about this?" or "What makes you angry about this
situation?" These questions encourage respondents to dig
deeper into their experiences and emotions, revealing more
profound and valuable insights.
3.Question
How can a researcher create a comfortable environment

for respondents?
Answer:To foster openness, establish a nonjudgmental
rapport with respondents. This involves listening actively,
showing empathy, and allowing silence so the interviewee
feels safe to share their thoughts freely. When people feel
listened to, they are more likely to share meaningful insights.
4.Question
What should a researcher remember when writing
insights?
Answer:Insights should be crafted with awareness of their
potential to surprise the audience. Use creative techniques
that introduce tension or a twist, helping the reader see
familiar concepts in a new light. For instance, juxtaposing
unrelated ideas can reveal surprising connections that enrich
understanding.
5.Question

generating insights?

6.Question
How can one define insights in a personal way?
Answer:You have the autonomy to define insights based on
your own experiences and interpretation. Whether you see
them as surprises, revelations, or turns in understanding,
defining them personally grants you ownership over the
insights you formulate and explore in your work.
