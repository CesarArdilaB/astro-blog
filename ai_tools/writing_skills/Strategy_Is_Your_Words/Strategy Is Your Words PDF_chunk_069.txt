Chapter 64 Summary : How Other
People Block Strategy
How Other People Block Strategy
Many strategists face internal creative blocks that can be
exacerbated by external influences. These blocks often stem
from the ambiguous nature of strategy, the status associated
with it, and the complexities of human dynamics in the
workplace.
Common Sentences that Block Strategists
1.
"You’re going too far."
- This often comes from a reluctance to engage deeply with
strategy, leading to vague language that avoids solving
specific problems.
2.
"I already did the strategy."

[PÁGINA 239]

- Some clients believe they have enough internal strategies,
making agency strategists feel undervalued or unnecessary.
3.
"What creative brief?"
- Sometimes, creative departments disregard the strategists,
working independently of the strategy or considering
themselves the strategists.
4.
"What’s a strategist?"
- As agencies grow, they may hire strategists without
understanding their role, leading to resistance when
strategists require time for research and accountability.
5.
"Sorry, we don’t need this strategy stuff."
- Certain clients prioritize established processes over
strategic intuition, fearing the unknown despite examples of
successful spontaneous ideas.
Ultimately, effective strategy requires a foundational
understanding of strategy itself.

Chapter 65 Summary : How to Know
When Strategy Has Happened
How to Know When Strategy Has Happened
To determine if a strategy has successfully emerged, one
should examine three core elements: mechanics, feelings, and
reactions.
Understanding Strategy in Context
- Identify the meaning of "strategy" within your specific
environment or project.
- Explore other words that complement the idea of strategy
and their significance.
- Gather insights on other people's perceptions and
expectations regarding strategy in the given context.
Testing the Mechanics of Strategy
- Assess if the strategy addresses a legitimate problem.
- Verify the existence of this problem through research.
