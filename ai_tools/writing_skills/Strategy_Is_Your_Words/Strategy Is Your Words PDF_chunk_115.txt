Chapter 19 | Feelings Are Clues| Q&A
1.Question
Why are feelings important in strategy work?
Answer:Feelings are crucial in strategy work
because they make both strategists and their
audiences more connected to the work being done.
Embracing feelings can lead to deeper insights and
more authentic connections, allowing for creativity
to flourish and strategies to resonate better with
people.
2.Question
How can denial of feelings hinder strategy development?
Answer:Denial of feelings can lead to superficial strategy
that lacks depth and genuine connection. When strategists
suppress emotions, they risk creating work that is uninspired
and fails to engage clients and colleagues, ultimately
resulting in frustration and a lack of effectiveness.
3.Question
What are the three waves of feeling that clients might
experience during a strategy presentation?

[PÁGINA 415]

Answer:The first wave is individual feelings, focusing on the
personal emotional reactions of clients. The second wave
considers how the final audience will feel about the work.
The third wave involves the client's responsibility towards
the strategy and how it makes them feel about their role in its
implementation.
4.Question
What should strategists do if a client expresses hesitation
about a presented strategy?
Answer:If a client shows hesitation, the strategist should
inquire 'What would need to happen for you to feel
differently?' This question helps uncover underlying feelings
and reasons, allowing for a deeper understanding and clearer
communication.
5.Question
What is the impact of feelings on the relationships
between strategists, colleagues, and clients?
Answer:When strategists and their peers engage with
feelings, it fosters richer conversations and a stronger

[PÁGINA 416]

connection, leading to more vibrant and lasting relationships.
Conversely, ignoring feelings can reduce interactions to mere
transactions, undermining the creative process and
satisfaction.
6.Question
What is the essence of good creative leadership in the
context of strategy?
Answer:Good creative leadership transcends managing a
department; it is about empowering the creativity of
everyone in the organization while honoring their unique
skills and contributions. This approach nurtures a
collaborative environment where strategy can thrive.

How can strategists turn negative feelings expressed by
clients into productive discussions?

than personal attacks. By asking probing questions about
