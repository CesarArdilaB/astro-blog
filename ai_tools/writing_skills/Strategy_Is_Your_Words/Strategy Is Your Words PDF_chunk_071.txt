Chapter 67 Summary : Communications
Frameworks –A Short Word
Communications Frameworks
A communications framework is essential after a campaign
idea emerges, helping to integrate the idea's presentation with
its intended audience and available resources. It addresses
key considerations about where and how a campaign idea
should be showcased.
Key Considerations for Campaign Ideas
-
Emotional Resonance:
If a campaign deals with specific emotions like anger or
intimacy, it should be positioned in environments that evoke
those feelings.
-
Audience Insight:
Identifying genres relevant to the campaign is crucial,
utilizing the target audience's behavior and preferences to

[PÁGINA 247]

shape media choices effectively.
Problems with Traditional Campaign Plans
1. Inefficient media plans focusing on efficiency rather than
effectiveness.
2. Ideas that are merely video scripts, lacking depth.
3. Overloading a single piece of communication with
excessive information.
Process Recommendations
- Develop a shortlist of suitable media channels based on the
target audience before crafting the creative brief.
- Use insights from prior campaigns and current marketing
science, while allowing creativity and intuition to guide final
decisions.
Analogies and Creative Insights
- Campaigns can be likened to personal endeavors, such as
finding love, requiring specific strategies to attract the
desired audience.
- The importance of understanding desires, behaviors, and

[PÁGINA 248]

environments similar to courting behaviors helps inform
better communication strategies.
Constructing a Communications Plan
Using the "Play with Death" campaign as an example:
-
Three-Act Structure:
Tease (capture attention), Launch (create an epic moment),
Sustain (maintain interest).
-
Campaign-Specific Language:
Utilize language that connects directly with the core
message and audience fears.
-
Addressing Objections:
Anticipate potential objections and frame responses to
reassure the audience, emphasizing relevance and
engagement.
Channel Selection
- Identify channels where the audience engages with their
fears or seeks solace, ensuring the campaign resonates with

[PÁGINA 249]

their experiences and needs.
Conclusion
Developing a robust communications framework enhances
the likelihood of connecting with the audience effectively,
optimizing budgets, and justifying creative work, ultimately
aiding in campaign success.
