Chapter 8 | Rejection Waits to Pounce| Q&A
1.Question
What does order seek, and how does it relate to ideas?
Answer:Order seeks to maintain stability and
predictability in society. It views ideas as threats
which disrupt this stability. When someone presents
a new idea, order reacts with suspicion, fearing the
potential chaos that may follow if those ideas gain
traction.
2.Question
How can feelings of loneliness be interpreted for a
strategist?
Answer:Loneliness often reflects a misalignment with the
self and one’s ideas. It's a symptom of not fully embracing
and befriending one’s thoughts. Instead of seeing it as a flaw,
a strategist can view it as a cue to create, explore, and seek
like-minded allies.

3.Question
Why is it essential for a strategist to take back their
story?
Answer:Reclaiming your narrative allows you to define your
identity and purpose, especially in a world that may label you
as defective. By asserting your story, you can connect with
others who resonate with your vision and collaborate in
expressing it.
4.Question
What role do coping mechanisms play in a strategist's
life?
Answer:Coping mechanisms can arise from the fear of social
rejection and the pressure to conform to societal norms. They
often lead to self-doubt and isolation, stifling creativity and
preventing open expression of ideas.
5.Question

discouraging the sharing of ideas. People worry about being

6.Question
How should a strategist cope with feelings of brokenness?
Answer:Instead of succumbing to the notion of being
irreparably broken, a strategist should embrace the ability to
reconstruct their identity from various pieces. This process of
self-reinvention is vital for maintaining a sense of wholeness
and purpose.
7.Question
What is the danger of pre-emptive self-rejection?
Answer:Pre-emptive self-rejection is a defense mechanism
that can lead to a false sense of control. By rejecting oneself
first, a strategist may think they are protecting themselves
from external rejection, but this mindset can ultimately
isolate them further and stifle their ideas.
