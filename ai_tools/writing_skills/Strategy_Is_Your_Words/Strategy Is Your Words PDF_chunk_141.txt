10.Question
What is implied by the phrase 'some people think you
need a license to have ideas'?
Answer:This phrase suggests that creativity is often stifled by
societal beliefs that restrict idea generation, when, in fact,
everyone has the capacity to generate ideas without needing

2.Question
How can the phrase 'ideas can come from anywhere' be
interpreted positively and negatively?
Answer:Positively, 'ideas can come from anywhere'
encourages individuals to recognize their innate creativity
and to embrace diverse perspectives, fostering innovation.
Negatively, it can be misused as an excuse to avoid hiring
skilled professionals or to diminish the value of experienced
idea generators, leading to superficial brainstorming without
real depth or quality.
3.Question
Why is equating the phrase 'everyone is creative' with the
American Dream problematic?
Answer:Equating 'everyone is creative' with the American
Dream is problematic because it suggests that creativity is
universally attainable, similar to the pursuit of happiness,
despite the reality that not everyone will excel in creative
endeavors. It creates unrealistic expectations and can lead to

[PÁGINA 501]

frustration when people realize that while they may dream of
being creative, very few will reach exceptional levels without
dedication and practice.
4.Question
What is the role of a 'creative strategist' in an
organization?
Answer:A 'creative strategist' serves to bridge the gap
between strategy and creativity, indicating their capacity to
think strategically while also engaging in creative processes.
It signals a senior position that values both strategic thinking
and creative input, though it should not diminish the notion
that all strategists need to engage in creativity as part of their
fundamental role.
