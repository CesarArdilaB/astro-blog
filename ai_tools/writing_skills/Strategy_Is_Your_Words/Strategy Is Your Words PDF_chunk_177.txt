1.Question
What are the key elements to determine if a strategy has

truly emerged?
Answer:The key elements to determine if a strategy
has emerged are mechanics, feelings, and reactions.
You assess the mechanics by ensuring the strategy
addresses a problem with solid research and
insights. Feelings gauge the emotional resonance and
originality of the strategy. Reactions assess how
others perceive and respond to the strategy, which
reflect its clarity and impact.
2.Question
How can I define 'strategy' in my context?
Answer:To define 'strategy' in your context, ask questions
like: What does 'strategy' mean for me in my life, company,
or project? What terms or concepts support the idea of
strategy? Engage with colleagues to understand their
definitions and needs regarding strategy, fostering a
collaborative understanding.
3.Question
What types of questions can help assess a strategy's

[PÁGINA 616]

effectiveness during evaluation?
Answer:Key questions include: 'Does this strategy try to
solve a clearly identified problem?' 'Is there a compelling
argument for how solving this problem will benefit the
business?' 'Does this strategy present a novel approach or
combination of ideas?' These questions guide your evaluation
process.
4.Question
What feelings should I look for when evaluating a
strategy?
Answer:When evaluating a strategy, look for feelings that
indicate excitement or intrigue, such as whether the strategy
feels 'right' in a positive sense, if it sparks new thoughts, or if
it carries energetic appeal. A strategy should feel fresh and
inspiring, not flat or repetitive.

6.Question
What is the ultimate goal of asking these evaluation
questions about strategy?
Answer:The ultimate goal of these evaluation questions is to
facilitate deeper understanding and clarity within the strategy
process. They help uncover underlying assumptions, validate
ideas, and inspire creativity, ultimately leading to a more
robust and impactful strategy.
