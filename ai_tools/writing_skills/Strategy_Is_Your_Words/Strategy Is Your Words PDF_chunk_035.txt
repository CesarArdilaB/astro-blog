The Importance of Openness
The piece emphasizes the necessity of maintaining an open
mind to foster more opinions and creativity. In the realm of
strategy, opinions should not be seen as right or wrong but as
starting points for deeper inquiries. It notes the dangers of
adhering strictly to accepted norms and the Overton Window,
which confines opinions to a predetermined set acceptable by
wider society.
Conclusion: The Art of Strategy
Ultimately, strategy is described as an ongoing interplay
between information and opinion, manifesting as drama and
art. The chapter concludes by noting that strategy should
embrace the fluidity of thought rather than lock itself into
rigid definitions, allowing for exploration and discovery in
pursuit of success.

Critical Thinking
Key Point:The subjectivity of strategy necessitates a

Critical Interpretation:Mark Pollard’s conception of
strategy as merely an informed opinion highlights a
significant tension in corporate environments, where
objectivity is often prioritized over subjective
perspectives. This viewpoint invites scrutiny,
particularly considering how prevailing assumptions can
stifle innovation if left unchallenged. Readers should
evaluate the validity of Pollard's assertions by referring
to resources such as "The Structure of Scientific
Revolutions" by Thomas S. Kuhn, which discusses the
shifts in paradigms that challenge established norms.
Thus, while Pollard advocates for treating opinions as
fluid starting points in strategy, one must remain
vigilant against the dangers of falling back into
entrenched beliefs, suggesting the need for a more
balanced perspective on the interplay of opinion and
evidence.
