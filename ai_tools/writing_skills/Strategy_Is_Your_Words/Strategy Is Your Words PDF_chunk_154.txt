8.Question
Why is careful thought about problems critical before
rushing to solutions?
Answer:Spending time to understand a problem thoroughly
often reveals the core issues needing resolution. When we
rush to solutions, we risk addressing symptoms rather than
root causes, potentially perpetuating the problem or creating
new challenges. The quote often attributed to Einstein
emphasizes the need for deep contemplation about problems
to raise the quality and impact of solutions.

1.Question
What is the significance of avoiding clichés in strategy
and creativity?

[PÁGINA 542]

Answer:Clichés lack originality and do not
demonstrate true creativity because they have
already been said and accepted. In strategy, using
clichés may oversimplify complex ideas and fail to
engage audiences meaningfully.
2.Question
How can we more effectively identify the 'human
problem' in our strategy?
Answer:To identify the 'human problem,' it is crucial to
clearly define the barrier preventing your audience from
taking desired actions. This definition should be concise,
avoiding jargon, to ensure clarity and focus.

4.Question

thorough problem discovery in strategy sessions?
Answer:Inviting a variety of viewpoints during strategy
sessions enriches the discussion, encourages introspection

may not be immediately apparent.

Answer:Understanding the distinction allows teams to
address the underlying issues rather than merely treating the
symptoms, which often leads to more effective and
sustainable solutions.
