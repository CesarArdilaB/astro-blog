Ask Your Way to the Edge
Understanding Strategy Through People
Discussing strategy inherently involves people. It’s essential
to view the brand from the perspective of potential buyers,
acknowledging that they live their lives often without
considering the brand. The challenge lies in redirecting
advertisers' focus from their brands to the consumers
purchasing them.
Defining a Brand
A brand is essentially how consumers perceive a product as
distinct from alternatives, reflecting their identities through
their choices. Developing a brand strategy involves
understanding which features and benefits resonate with the
target audience. It's about identifying a unique advantage that

[PÁGINA 212]

motivates buyers, rather than just a point of discussion in
meetings.
The Four Points Framework
In formulating strategy, one must succinctly express the
brand's advantage. This encourages clarity, boldness in
writing, and prioritization of ideas over lengthy bullet points.
A key question to address is, “What matters most to our
audience?”
Interviewing Consumers
To uncover the brand advantage, conduct interviews with
current users by asking targeted questions about their
purchasing decisions, product use, and perceptions.
Exploring consumer reviews can also provide insights,
although word clouds may not capture the depth of sentiment
effectively.
Articulating the Advantage
When conveying the brand's advantage, it’s crucial to go
beyond superficial traits and create a strong connection

[PÁGINA 213]

between the brand and the product. Effective communication
hinges on this relationship; without it, the messaging may
fall flat.
The Role of Communications Plans
A well-structured communications plan facilitates effective
discussion within teams about various marketing efforts,
ensuring that energy is appropriately distributed across
different strategies such as visual representation,
explanations, demonstrations, and endorsements. Utilizing
frameworks can enhance this exploration further.

The Pyramid of Advantage
Overview of Advantage in Marketing
The concept of "advantage" combines various marketing
ideas, emphasizing the notion of "unfair advantage," which
refers to unique qualities that set a company apart, such as
patents. Another aspect is "reasons to believe" (RTBs), which
substantiate a brand's main selling proposition.
Single-Minded Proposition and RTBs
- Example: “The New York Knicks will give you a hit of
New York rage.”
- RTBs: Documenting supportive points that validate the
proposition.
