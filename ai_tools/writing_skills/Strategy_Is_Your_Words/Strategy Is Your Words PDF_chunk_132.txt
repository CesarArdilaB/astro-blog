Chapter 32 | All Ideas Cross the Line| Q&A
1.Question
What does it mean to 'walk the line' in the context of
ideas and loyalty?
Answer:Walking the line refers to the practice of
sticking closely to conventional thoughts and ideas
related to a topic. For instance, thinking of night
would lead you to classic associations like 'dinner,'
'bedtime,' and 'dreams.' It embodies loyalty to
commonly known meanings and keeps the thoughts
linear, not venturing into the unexpected or
imaginative.
2.Question
How can lateral thinking enhance creativity?

[PÁGINA 471]

Answer:Lateral thinking allows one to combine disparate
ideas into something new and innovative. For example,
merging 'sunset' and 'factory' to create 'Sunset Factory,' an
all-glass restaurant with stunning views, demonstrates how
lateral thinking pushes boundaries and fosters unique
concepts that traditional linear thinking would overlook.
3.Question
What are some examples of how silent adjectives can
complicate communication?
Answer:Silent adjectives such as 'creative,' 'strategic,' or
'digital' can add layers of ambiguity to discussions about
ideas. For example, saying 'creative idea' lacks clarity about
what that entails, leading to confusion. Clear communication
is crucial to avoid misunderstandings, especially in
collaborative environments.

Why is it dangerous for teams to use jargon without
defining it?
