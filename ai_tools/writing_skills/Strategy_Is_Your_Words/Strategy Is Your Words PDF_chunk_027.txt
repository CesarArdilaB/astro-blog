Art Is in the Margins
Overview of Business Art
The author posits that what is created within the confines of
business is not genuine art but rather "business art" that
serves capitalism. Real art exists solely for itself and is often
misunderstood until its creator's demise. The focus is on

[PÁGINA 75]

generating profit and delivering ideas to clients, often
sidelining the true essence of art.
The Dichotomy of Strategy and Art
While art embodies uncertainty and emotional depth, science
strives for concrete truths. The author argues that strategy,
which utilizes facts to create concepts, is more akin to art
than to science. Award-winning papers reflect a blend of
imagination and facts—reinforcing the strategist's role as an
artist who can reshape perceptions and influence thoughts.
Perceptions and Challenges in Strategy
Many in the corporate world resist seeing strategy as art due
to concerns about credibility and the fear of losing their
identity in a domain that has traditionally leaned toward
science. This can lead to a professional identity crisis for
strategists who may see themselves as creatives in their
personal lives but not in their work environment.
