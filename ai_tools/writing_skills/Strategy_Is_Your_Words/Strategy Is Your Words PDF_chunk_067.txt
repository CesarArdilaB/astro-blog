Strategy Is Important but Whose Is Most
Important?
The significance of the term "strategy" is contentious in
various fields, particularly in business and advertising.
Different groups claim ownership over the term, leading to
confusion. For some, "strategy" is exclusively for business
professionals, while others prefer "account planning" to
describe the role of enhancing advertising effectiveness
through human insights. However, "account planning" lacks
the stature of "strategy," particularly in less experienced
environments, leading to perceived lower status.
The Cold War of Titles
This conflict has resulted in the emergence of specialized job
titles to assert the value and distinctiveness of various
strategic roles, such as "digital strategist" and "brand

[PÁGINA 232]

strategist." These titles are often arguments for higher
compensation. Contrarily, the title "client strategist" tends to
suggest a multifunctional role, often positioned as a balance
between strategic thought and salesmanship.
Passive-Aggressive Behavior Among Strategists
The competition creates an atmosphere of passive
aggression, where strategists may face resistance from
colleagues who attempt to overshadow their contributions.
This manifests through avoidance of meetings, disinterest in
collaborative discussions, and even the creation of new job
titles to reclaim importance.
A Cold War for Importance
Overall, this cold war over the definition and importance of
"strategy" reflects a larger struggle for recognition and
financial gain, rather than a pursuit of clarity concerning
what the term actually signifies within each context.
