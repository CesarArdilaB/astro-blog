Problem-Centric Strategies

Problems as Motivators
- A strong problem statement encourages action and
innovation and can serve as a catalyst for change.
- Recognizing human elements behind business issues
enhances empathy and offers new perspectives in
problem-solving.
Circular vs. Empathetic Problem Statements
- Circular statements often lead to unproductive discussions
and avoid the real issues.
- Empathetic statements identify genuine human challenges,
encouraging deeper understanding and relevant solutions.
Conclusion

Summary of Chapter 47: Problem Tools
Overview of Problem Identification Tools
Chapter 47 discusses various tools for identifying and
addressing problems in strategic thinking and marketing,
primarily emphasizing the need to go beyond superficial
solutions.
Convenient Clichés
The weakest level of creativity involves the use of clichés,
which lack originality and often lead to vague
communication. Agencies tend to rely on "Convenient
Clichés" to appeal to a broad audience, particularly in
politics.
All the Stuff We Can Make
This point critiques the tendency of agencies to present a

Taglines and Manifestos
While taglines and manifestos hold historical significance
and can resonate powerfully, they may not adapt well across
various media channels, especially if derived without a
strong campaign idea.
Finding the Human Problem
The chapter stresses the importance of identifying the
"human problem," which is the specific barrier preventing an
audience from engaging with a message. This approach
requires a deep understanding of the underlying issues rather
than just surface-level symptoms.
