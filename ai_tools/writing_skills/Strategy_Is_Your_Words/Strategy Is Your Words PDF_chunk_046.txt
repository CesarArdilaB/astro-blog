Silent Adjectives Are Slow Deaths
Silence and Observation
Silence can bring clarity, allowing one to experience life
through mindfulness. Observing the world provides a deeper
understanding of human interactions and creativity.
However, silence can also lead to stagnation within teams.
When jargon is undefined and meanings are unspoken, it can
stifle communication and collaboration.
The Death of Meaning
When teams fail to articulate the meaning of their jargon or
the value of strategy, it creates confusion and diminishes the
role of strategists. Questions that feign interest are
detrimental to growth, and a lack of feedback can stifle
creativity. The absence of discussion around team dynamics
and goals can lead to team suicide, ignoring the human
element essential for effective teamwork.
The Problem with Silent Adjectives
Silent adjectives, often laden with unnecessary complexity,

complicate communication. Words like “creative,” “digital,”
or “strategic” can obscure meaning instead of enhancing it.
This leads to murky conversations where essential ideas get
lost, suffocating creative exploration within teams.
Ideas Come Big and Small
The Illusion of Size
The tendency to label ideas as “big” or “small” can be
misleading and counterproductive. Doing so implies a
hierarchy that may not exist, reducing the value of innovative
concepts. Every idea should be considered for its merit rather
than its perceived size.
Consequences of “Big” Ideas
The demand for a “big idea” often translates to inflated
expectations and pressure to overdeliver, which can stifle
creativity. Such phrases serve as code for emphasizing the
importance of a project and the stature of the individuals
involved, rather than promoting originality and artistic
expression.
