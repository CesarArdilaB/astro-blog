Chapter 22 | Opinions Are Everywhere| Q&A
1.Question
What is the true nature of strategy according to the
author?
Answer:Strategy is not merely a collection of facts;
it is an informed opinion about how to win, created
from a blend of data, information, and creative
thinking. It's like cooking where raw ingredients
(facts) are transformed into a delicious meal
(strategy) through the chef's (strategist's)
perspective.
2.Question
Why do people often reject or minimize others' opinions?
Answer:People often diminish others' opinions to assert
dominance and reinforce their own egos. By indicating 'that's
just your opinion,' they attempt to reduce the other person's
influence and validate their own stance.
3.Question
In what context does the concept of 'subjectivity' come
into play for business people?

[PÁGINA 429]

Answer:Business people often dislike subjectivity as they
value objective facts. They prefer information dressed as
absolute truth, masking their own subjective opinions which
are inherently part of their decision-making process.
4.Question
How can opinions lead to new ideas?
Answer:Opinions can branch out into new thoughts and
solutions by encouraging dialogue and exploration. They
serve as starting points for deeper discussions, leading to a
divergence in thinking once an open-minded approach is
adopted.
5.Question
What role do comedians play in the exploration of
opinion?
Answer:Comedians utilize strong opinions in their premises,
creating tension and dramatic narratives. They skillfully
navigate through absurd combinations of ideas to provoke
thought, entertain, and ultimately deliver a resolution,
highlighting the creative potential of opinions.

[PÁGINA 430]

6.Question
What happens when an opinion masquerades as a fact?
Answer:When opinions are dressed as facts, they can mislead
people and stifle further questioning. This is particularly
problematic in media and science, where the appearance of
absolute truth can dominate public discourse and limit
creative or critical thinking.
7.Question
Why is it important to understand the fluidity of
opinions?
Answer:Understanding that opinions can evolve and lead to
more questions is crucial for innovation and progress. It
encourages an open-minded environment where new ideas
can flourish rather than confining thinking to rigid binaries of
right and wrong.
