The 5 Whys Technique
Developed by Sakichi Toyoda, this method involves
repeatedly asking "why" to peel back layers of a problem
until the root cause is revealed. This helps in developing
insights that drive effective solutions.

Insights as Survival Tools
Insights play a crucial role in human survival, acting as life
and death tools that arise from gossip and stories. They must
compete against one another to remain relevant, highlighting
the survival instinct inherent in humanity.
Definition and Nature of Insights
Insights are defined as unspoken human truths that illuminate
problems. They reveal overlooked aspects of reality and
often evoke personal confessions and inner voices,
compelling individuals to confront challenging truths. They
are intertwined with irony and melancholy, reflecting the
complexities of human existence.
The Creative Process of Insights
Insights should be approached playfully rather than

[PÁGINA 189]

methodically. They are akin to ideas and encompass infinite
potential, as new experiences can yield new insights at any
moment.
Distinguishing Ideas from Insights
The distinction between an idea and an insight lies in the
latter's capacity to inspire life changes, pushing individuals to
reorganize their lives rather than merely presenting a
combination of thoughts.
Application of Insights in Strategy
For insights to be actionable, they must be refined and
contextualized to address specific business issues. Strategists
must determine whether to align with or challenge the
insight, leading to strategic directions that resonate with the
target audience.

The Difficulty with Insights
Understanding Insights
Insights are often misunderstood in various ways:
-
Not Numbers
: Insights explain the reasons behind numbers, not the
numbers themselves.
-
Not Reports
: They synthesize data from reports into human-focused
statements.
-
Not Facts or Judgements
: Insights question facts, prioritizing a compassionate
understanding over judgment.
