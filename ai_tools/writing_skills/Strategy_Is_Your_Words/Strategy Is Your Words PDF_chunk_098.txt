Chapter 6 | Fog Is Clarity Coming| Q&A
1.Question
What does the author suggest about the nature of a
lifelong quest for meaning?

[PÁGINA 358]

Answer:It is complex and chaotic rather than
straightforward. The author compares it to trying to
keep track of an object in an industrial washing
machine, highlighting the ups and downs and the
confusion that often accompanies the journey.
2.Question
How does the author differentiate between fog and smog?
Answer:Fog represents temporary confusion and is part of
the process of thinking deeply, while smog symbolizes the
negative outcomes of poor thinking habits and decisions that
can lead to crises and misunderstandings.
3.Question
What is the significance of the Clean Air Act metaphor in
the context of thinking and strategy?
Answer:The Clean Air Act represents principles and
practices that help maintain clarity in thought. It promotes
the idea of separating useful thoughts (fog) from toxic ones
(smog) to ensure productive and meaningful strategizing.
4.Question
What types of thoughts does the author identify, and how

[PÁGINA 359]

should we manage them?
Answer:The author identifies nonsense thoughts, histrionics,
quips, and classic observations. A constructive approach
involves recognizing their potential utility, treating them as
raw material for insight, and applying discernment in their
usage rather than rejecting them outright.
5.Question
What questions does the author recommend to clarify our
strategic intentions?
Answer:The author suggests asking questions such as: 'Are
we here to do great work?' 'What is that?' 'How do we believe
it happens?' 'What are our constraints?' and 'How can we be
inventive in the midst of our constraints?' These questions
help reset the focus and clarity when navigating through fog.
6.Question
What are some practical activities the author suggests for
maintaining clarity and engaging with thinking?
Answer:Practical activities include getting outside, engaging
with nature, changing environments, writing or drawing,

[PÁGINA 360]

listening to music, exercising, discussing ideas with friends,
and expressing gratitude. These actions stimulate creativity
and help keep the mind and body balanced.
7.Question
How does the author suggest we view the process of
thinking amidst fog?
Answer:The author encourages us to appreciate the nature of
thinking as often being clouded by fog, acknowledging it as a
normal part of the cognitive process. The challenge is to
cultivate awareness and practices that keep our thinking clear
and avoid the dangers of 'smog'.
8.Question
What can be the consequences of allowing 'smog' to
influence strategy work?
Answer:Allowing smog to dominate can lead to confusion,
miscommunication, and ultimately, a decline in one's
strategic career. The author emphasizes that clear thinking is
crucial for effective strategy.
