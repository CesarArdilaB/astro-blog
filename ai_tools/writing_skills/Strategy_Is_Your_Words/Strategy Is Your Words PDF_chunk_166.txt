3.Question
How can RTBs create marketing challenges?
Answer:Common challenges include: 1. Technical jargon
that confuses consumers. 2. A list of untested ideas that lack
cohesion or relevance to the target audience. 3. A failure to
empathize with what potential buyers are actually interested
in.
4.Question
What are the parts of the Pyramid of Advantage?
Answer:The Pyramid of Advantage consists of three parts: 1.
Must Have - essential features a product or service needs to
even be considered. 2. Nice to Have - additional features that
are appealing but not required for purchase. 3. Love to Have
- standout features that excite customers and make them
willing to pay.

6.Question
What differentiates 'High Involvement' and 'Low
Involvement' products?
Answer:High involvement products are ones where
consumers invest significant time and emotions in the
purchasing decision, like cars or vacations, while low
involvement products are purchased with little thought or
emotional investment, like snacks or laundry detergent.
7.Question
Why is it important to consider the category of product
use cases?
Answer:Exploring use cases encourages thinking about the
various reasons consumers might choose a product, revealing
potential competition and areas to innovate, ultimately
leading to a better understanding of customer needs.

unique selling points?
