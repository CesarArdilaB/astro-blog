Chapter 59 Summary : The Decision
Funnel
The Decision Funnel
The Decision Funnel translates research findings into a
structured representation of consumer decision-making
processes surrounding a product or service. This framework
emphasizes the importance of understanding that
decision-making is rarely linear or perfect; instead, it reflects
the unpredictable nature of human thought and behavior.
Understanding the Decision Funnel
- Recognizes the varied influences on consumer choices,
acknowledging that different people prioritize different
arguments in their decision-making.
- Encourages empathy and helps identify potential blind
spots in understanding the audience.
Case Study: CEO's Decision Funnel for Hiring a
New Head of Planning

[PÁGINA 227]

- Acknowledges the need for change after losing a Head of
Planning.
- Outlines recruitment steps, including gathering opinions
from management and utilizing both internal and external
recruiters.
- Describes a meticulous weekly shortlist review and
conversations with potential hires.
- Details the interview process, including separate meetings
with the management and strategy teams to evaluate fit.
- Concludes the process with an offer stage, decision
timeframe, and preparation for the new hire’s arrival while
highlighting the ongoing challenges faced by the new hire in
integrating with a resistant team.
This organized approach showcases the complexities
involved in decision-making within a corporate context,
demonstrating the iterative nature of both hiring and the
broader decision-making landscape.

Chapter 60 Summary : Path to Purchase
Path to Purchase
The Path to Purchase outlines how consumers transition from
unawareness of a product to making a purchase, which can
be approached broadly or tailored to specific audiences. It
serves as both a map of existing behaviors and a plan for
strategic marketing activities, depending on the questions
posed.
