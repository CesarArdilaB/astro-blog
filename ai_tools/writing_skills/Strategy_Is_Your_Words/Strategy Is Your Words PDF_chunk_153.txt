2.Question
What are the three genres of selling identified in the text,
and how do they differ?

[PÁGINA 538]

Answer:1. **Feature-selling**: Focuses solely on the
qualities of a product or service (e.g., 'Our software has this
great feature').
2. **Needs-based selling**: Aims to uncover the client's
problems to offer solutions (e.g., 'Can we meet to discover
your issues? Here's how our product solves them').
3. **Problem-based selling**: Proactively emphasizes the
problems that need attention, framing the sale around the
customer's pain points rather than just solutions. Examples
include brands like Dollar Shave Club, which highlight
industry issues rather than just product features.

4.Question
What does a good problem statement look like, and why
is it important?
Answer:A good problem statement is compelling and
creative, like a dare—encouraging action and innovative
thinking. It should be focused on real human experiences,
moving beyond circular reasoning that often halts progress.
For example, instead of saying 'We aren't selling enough', a
more human-centric approach like 'Fans are hate-supporting
the New York Knicks' can catalyze more impactful
conversations and solutions.
