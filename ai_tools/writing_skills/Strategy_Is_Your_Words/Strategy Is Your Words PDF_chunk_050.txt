The Ideas of a Strategist
Strategists, much like nonfiction writers, must observe the
world uniquely and present compelling insights to engage
their audience. Effective writing is essential for success in
strategy, demanding a deep understanding of research, which
often faces challenges due to time constraints in today's
fast-paced business environment.
The Importance of Research
Strategists in the past had ample time—sometimes
months—to create creative briefs. Today, clients have their
own research teams, and the quality of research varies
significantly. Solid research input ensures honesty and
relevance to the target audience.
Crafting Strong Ideas

Writing is vital not only for clarity but also for the strength of
ideas presented. For example, transforming a statement like
“We need to increase awareness of our underpants among
teenagers” into a more compelling narrative can significantly
impact the strategy's effectiveness. Ideas should provoke
thought and resonate with the audience, moving beyond
superficial claims.
Defining Ideas and Creativity
An idea is a unique combination of seemingly unrelated
elements. The distinction between a strategist’s ideas and
those of creatives can be blurred, but strategists should aim to
inspire creative teams while allowing room for their input.
Concepts termed “territories”—broad themes like
“simplicity” or “empowerment”—can sometimes hinder
decision-making rather than enhance it.
