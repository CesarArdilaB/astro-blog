Chapter 34 | Ideas Come Big and Small| Q&A
1.Question
What defines a 'big idea' in the context of creativity and
innovation?
Answer:A 'big idea' is often perceived as one that
carries more weight, complexity, or potential for
impact compared to a 'small idea.' However, the
differentiation can be misleading, as both big and
small ideas can be equally valuable. The true
measure of an idea's 'size' may lie in its novelty,
usefulness, and the breadth of its application.
2.Question
Why might people call for a 'big idea' in discussions
about creativity?
Answer:When someone requests a 'big idea,' it usually
conveys the importance and urgency of the project at hand.
It's a way to signal that the expectation is for innovative and
transformative thinking, especially in a competitive or
high-stakes environment.

[PÁGINA 482]

3.Question
Can a small idea be impactful?
Answer:Absolutely. A small idea can be revolutionary if it
addresses a significant problem or concern, leading to
profound changes despite its seemingly lesser scale. For
example, a small tweak in a product can enhance user
experience dramatically.
4.Question
How does the context influence whether an idea is
perceived as big or small?
Answer:The context in which an idea is presented plays a
critical role in its perception. Factors such as the audience's
needs, the resources available, and the nature of the problem
being addressed can all shift the view of an idea's scale.
