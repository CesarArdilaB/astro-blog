Chapter 64 | How Other People Block Strategy|
Q&A
1.Question
What is one of the major challenges strategists face in
their work?
Answer:Many strategists experience internal
creative blocks, often exacerbated by others who
may not understand or support the nuanced work of
strategy.
2.Question
How can vague language be detrimental to the creation of
effective strategies?
Answer:Vague notions and terms like 'performance' or
'durability' dilute the specificity and focus needed in strategy,
causing it to lose its power and impact.
3.Question
Why might other departments within a company dismiss
the value of strategists?
Answer:Some departments, particularly those with strong
resources like MBAs or consumer insight groups, may

[PÁGINA 613]

believe they already understand strategy, leading to a
disconnect and undervaluation of the strategist's role.
4.Question
What can happen to a strategy after a briefing if not
managed correctly?
Answer:After a briefing, a strategy can quickly dissolve into
obscurity, with creative departments sometimes ignoring it
entirely in favor of their own processes.
5.Question
How do some companies approach the concept of strategy
in a narrow way?
Answer:Some companies may rely on rigid processes or
proprietary methods to justify their work, often overlooking
the intuitive and organic aspects of developing a successful
strategy.
6.Question
What does the phrase 'It Just Came To Me™' imply
about the nature of idea generation?
Answer:It suggests a dismissive view of the strategic process,
reducing the depth and complexity of developing ideas to

[PÁGINA 614]

mere chance rather than acknowledging the research,
thought, and effort that typically underpin successful
strategies.
7.Question
In what way can the relationship between strategists and
clients impact the final output of a campaign?
Answer:If clients do not respect or understand the strategic
input, strategists may find their insights sidelined, leading to
outputs that lack cohesion and effectiveness because they are
not informed by solid strategies.
8.Question
How can strategists combat the blocks created by others?
Answer:Strategists can advocate for the importance of clarity
and specificity in strategy, and work to educate other
departments on the necessity of collaborative, strategic
thinking.
