Chapter 19 Summary : Feelings Are

Feelings Are Clues
The Role of Feelings in Strategy
Strategy evokes strong emotions, both for strategists and
their colleagues, clients, and the public. Emotions in strategy
are often dismissed in favor of cold, quantitative analysis, but
this perspective limits true understanding. Embracing
feelings is crucial, as they provide insights and drive
creativity.
Empathy and Internal Feelings
Strategists experience a range of feelings throughout a
project—from excitement and clarity to confusion and guilt.
These feelings help strategists gain deeper personal insights
and navigate the complexities of strategy work.

[PÁGINA 86]

Colleague Reactions to Strategy
The use of the term “strategy” can lead to resentment among
colleagues, particularly when it is overused or misused. Good
creative leadership magnifies rather than diminishes the
contributions of all team members, fostering a culture of
creativity and respect.
Client Emotions and Strategy
When engaging with clients, it’s essential to address their
feelings. Clients experience emotions at multiple levels about
the work presented, and understanding these responses can
reveal how to refine the strategy and improve client
relationships.
Navigating Client Interactions
Tackling client emotions directly can lead to fruitful
discussions and deeper relationships. Recognizing and
addressing client feelings—whether positive or
negative—can illuminate the path to collaboration and better
outcomes.

[PÁGINA 87]

Conclusion
Incorporating feelings into strategy leads to a more vibrant
and engaging work life, transforming strategy from a
transactional process into an art form that enhances
relationships and drives success. Embracing emotions in
strategic discussions fosters creativity and connection,
contrasting sharply with a sterile, report-driven approach to
strategy.

[PÁGINA 88]

Example
Key Point:Embrace emotions for effective strategy.
Example:As you tackle your next project, imagine
presenting your strategy to colleagues, not with just
numbers, but with heartfelt stories that reflect the team's
journey. Feel the room shift as you share your
excitement about potential outcomes, while
acknowledging the fear of failure. As your colleagues
resonate with these emotions, they become more
invested, sparking creativity and collaboration. Instead
of a sterile report, your strategy becomes a shared
vision, driven by empathy and genuine connection,
proving that understanding feelings is essential for
memorable and impactful outcomes.
