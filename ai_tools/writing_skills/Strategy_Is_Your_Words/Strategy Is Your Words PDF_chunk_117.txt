6.Question
How can taking risks in presentations lead to better
outcomes?
Answer:Taking risks in presentations can elevate the stakes
and spark audience interest. By trying a new approach or
incorporating unexpected elements, presenters can break
through monotony, maintain engagement, and increase the
likelihood of achieving a favorable response. It shifts the
experience from a mundane report to a captivating story.
7.Question
What does drama in strategy work say about the
strategist's role?
Answer:Drama in strategy work underscores the strategist's
role as a storyteller and dramatist. Strategists are responsible
for observing the world, crafting narratives that capture the
human experience, and ensuring that the strategies resonate
on an emotional level—beyond mere data.

embracing drama in strategy?

audience’s needs and limits.

Chapter 21 | Information Can Crush You| Q&A
1.Question
How can information be misused, and what consequences
does it have on creativity?
Answer:Information, when wielded by those with
malicious intent, operates as a tool for suppression
rather than enlightenment. It seeks to eradicate
uncertainty and stifle independent thinking, thus
creating a hostile environment for creativity. This
misuse prevents the evolution of art and ideas, as it
turns creative urges into mere conformity to
data-driven formulas, ultimately crushing the
human impulse to innovate.
