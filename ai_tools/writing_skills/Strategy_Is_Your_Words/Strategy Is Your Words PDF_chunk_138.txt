8.Question
Why should companies be prepared to define their ideas
for each new project?
Answer:Companies should be prepared to define their ideas
for each new project because new team members may not
share the same background knowledge or terminology. This
ensures everyone is on the same page, reducing confusion
and increasing the efficiency of the creative process. It also
allows for fresh perspectives while maintaining a foundation
that aligns with the brand’s goals and strategies.

1.Question
What is a business idea, and how does it relate to product
features?
Answer:A business idea is explained as something
novel a company does. It often begins with a product
feature that addresses a problem with an existing
product; however, a product feature alone may not
be sustainable as it can be easily replicated by
existing companies. For instance, a website
collecting Halloween movies represents a product
feature more than a standalone product.
2.Question
How can a business idea evolve into a more compelling
concept?
Answer:A business idea can evolve through lateral thinking,

subscription box service combining Halloween-themed items
and movies. Questions to explore include scalability, unfair
advantage, and potential to generate additional products,
helping the idea morph into a viable business model.
3.Question
What are the essential components of a brand essence,
purpose, promise, and idea?
Answer:Brand essence is a succinct phrase that captures the
core of a brand, while brand purpose explains how the
business serves humanity and why employees work there. A
brand promise communicates the key assurance to customers,
and a brand idea encapsulates the essence, purpose, and
promise, guiding overall brand strategy.
