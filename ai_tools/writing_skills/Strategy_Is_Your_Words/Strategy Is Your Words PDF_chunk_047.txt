Chapter 34 Summary : Ideas Come Big
and Small
Ideas Come Big and Small
In this chapter, the necessity of differentiating between "big"
and "small" ideas in the creative process is questioned. The
use of adjectives to describe ideas reflects personal opinions,
imagined reactions from others, or expectations surrounding
funding and audacity. Labeling an idea as "big" implies the
existence of a "small" idea and fosters a hierarchy that may
not be beneficial.
The Importance of the "Big Idea"
The emphasis on "big ideas" creates pressure for ideas to
appear overly significant, akin to monumental projects that
seem impossible to manage. This expectation often leads to
professionals striving to present ideas so grand that they
become unmanageable, thus reinforcing a cycle of unrealistic
creative demands.

[PÁGINA 143]

Defining Size and Impact of Ideas
The chapter raises complex questions regarding what makes
an idea "big" or "small." It challenges the reader to consider
if the size of an idea relates to its utility, the breadth of its
impact, or the novelty it brings to the table. The discussion
juxtaposes various innovations from Facebook to the printing
press, emphasizing that the significance of an idea is often
subjective and contextual.
The Code Behind "Big Ideas"
The chapter elaborates that the phrase "we need a big idea"
often serves as a code for various organizational pressures,
signaling crucial projects or the need for improved effort. It
suggests that clients may not actually crave monumental
creativity; instead, they are looking for effective solutions to
pressing challenges.
Conclusion
Ultimately, the chapter concludes that the binary distinction
of "big" and "small" may lack utility and that true creative
work should focus on the quality and relevance of ideas

"big idea" is crucial for guiding teams toward meaningful
and impactful creative output.

Chapter 35 Summary : The Types of
Ideas in Advertising
The Types of Ideas in Advertising
1. Business Idea
What the company does that’s novel.
2. Product Idea
Something novel that people can buy from the company.
3. Brand Essence
A vague phrase that anchors the brand strategy.
4. Brand Purpose
How the company serves humanity.

[PÁGINA 146]

5. Brand Promise
What the brand promises to deliver to its customers.
6. Brand Idea
What the brand stands for that’s novel.
7. Brand Platform
An idea the brand can stand on for a good length of time.
8. Brand Tagline
A short phrase that brings to life what the brand stands for.
9. Advertising Idea
An idea that repeats in every communication across all
channels.
10. Campaign Idea
A subset and shorter-term execution of the advertising idea
