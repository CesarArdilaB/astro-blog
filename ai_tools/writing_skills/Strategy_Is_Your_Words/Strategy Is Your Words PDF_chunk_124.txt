6.Question
How does uncertainty in a strategist's thinking manifest
in their presentations?
Answer:When strategists are unsure of their own ideas, they
may overcompensate by producing lengthy presentations
filled with information in hopes that something will resonate.
This often leads to a cacophony instead of a clear,
memorable message.
7.Question
What is Pollard’s view on double-entendres in strategy?
Answer:While double-entendres and wordplay can be
effective, they should be used deliberately and with clarity.
Strategists need to ensure that the intended meaning is the
focus, rather than trying to convey multiple interpretations
that could confuse the audience.
8.Question
In what way is writing critical to a strategist's success?
Answer:Writing is essential for strategists because it forces
them to clarify their thoughts and refine their messages into
understandable and impactful language. Strong writing skills

that resonate with the audience.
9.Question
What is the primary goal for a strategist according to this
chapter?
Answer:The primary goal is to distill strategy into a single,
clear note of meaning that can capture attention and be
memorable. The focus should be on ensuring that the
message is understood rather than trying to impress with
complexity.
10.Question
Why is cutting unnecessary words important in strategy?
Answer:Cutting unnecessary words is crucial because it helps
avoid clutter and confusion, allowing the message to shine
through with clarity. By being succinct, strategists can
eliminate noise and enhance the memorable impact of their
communication.
