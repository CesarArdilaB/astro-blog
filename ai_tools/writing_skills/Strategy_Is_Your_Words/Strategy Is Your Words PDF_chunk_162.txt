8.Question
What are the misconceptions some strategists have about
asking questions?
Answer:Some strategists mistakenly believe that asking
questions reflects weakness or a lack of knowledge, feeling
pressured to provide answers instead. Others may see
research strictly through an academic lens, adhering to rigid
methodologies that prevent them from exploring more
creative or intuitive questioning approaches.

both involve uncovering hidden truths and recognizing
unexpected connections within one's experiences. Just as an
insight can redefine previously held beliefs, personal growth

Chapter 53 | How to Help a Workshop Write an
Insight| Q&A
1.Question
How can teams effectively generate insights during
workshops instead of forcing them in a public setting?
Answer:Teams should focus on using methods that
allow ideas to flourish privately. Creativity often
requires time away from group dynamics, where
individuals can take risks, explore tangents, and
refine their thoughts without the pressure of an
audience. Encouraging participants to gather stimuli
in public but then retreat to think and edit in private
leads to stronger insights.
2.Question
What techniques can be used to encourage participants to
express insights more creatively?
Answer:Using conversational techniques like asking 'You
know how?' or 'It’s like...' prompts attendees to connect
personal experiences with deeper truths. These phrases can

[PÁGINA 569]

open up a dialogue that allows a more authentic voice to
emerge, facilitating a shift from formal language to more
relatable expressions.
3.Question
Why is it important to let participants have a 'breathing
space' during workshops?
Answer:Breathing space allows individuals to process their
thoughts and emotions without the crowd's pressure. This
space can lead to more genuine insights, as participants are
less likely to conform to expected roles and can embrace
vulnerability in sharing their thoughts.
4.Question
How does humor play a role in helping teams develop
insights?
Answer:Humor can help to lighten the atmosphere and shift
perspectives, allowing participants to approach problems
from new angles. Techniques like 'Say it like a comedian'
encourage creative expression, making insights feel more
digestible and relatable.
