Thinking About Uniqueness
Products can be assessed either linearly (unique features) or
laterally (unique combinations). The "Pyramid of
Advantage" aids in determining dramatic aspects of a product
from the audience's perspective.
Components of the Pyramid of Advantage
1.
Must Have:
Essential features for initial consideration (e.g., a car must
have four wheels).
2.
Nice to Have:
Attractive features that are not critical for purchase decisions
(e.g., rearview cameras in cars).
3.
Love to Have:
Must-have features that excite customers enough to buy
(e.g., competitive sports teams).

[PÁGINA 216]

Use Cases and Market Segmentation
Understanding different use cases for a product can reveal its
perceived value to consumers, highlighting how attributes
can shift in relevance:
-
Basketball Must-Haves:
Essential elements like tickets and merchandise.
-
Basketball Nice-to-Haves:
Additional attractions that vary in importance among
different audience segments.
-
Basketball Love-to-Haves:
Competitive teams that excite but may not be essential for
attendance.
Evolution of Customer Needs
As customer preferences evolve, so do the essential and
attractive features of products. It’s crucial to identify missing
attributes that could be significant to consumers.

[PÁGINA 217]

Marketing Strategies for Non-Unique Products
In cases where uniqueness is absent:
1. Rely on temporary strategies (discounts).
2. Creative messaging that is not overly obvious.
3. Unrelated insights for engagement.
4. Consider changing the product or job.
5. Innovate to add differentiation without deception.

Example
Key Point:The importance of identifying unique
attributes that resonate with consumers lies in the
Pyramid of Advantage.
Example:Imagine you are launching a new smartphone.
To stand out in a crowded market, you must first assess
the must-have features customers expect, like a
long-lasting battery and high-quality camera. Then, you
enhance your product with nice-to-have elements, such
as a distinctive design or user-friendly interface, that
attract attention. Finally, to truly captivate potential
buyers, integrate love-to-have innovations, like
augmented reality capabilities, which create buzz and
excitement. By mapping these features along the
Pyramid of Advantage, you ensure your offering is not
just another option but a compelling choice that meets
evolving consumer desires.
