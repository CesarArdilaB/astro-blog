Problems Can Do Good
The Power of Problems
- Problems are integral to human stories and narratives,
captivating our attention and shaping our experiences.
- They are inherent in our conversations and belief systems,
reflecting our struggles and aspirations.
Human Nature and Business Structures
- Companies were created to address human concerns, yet
they often distance themselves from the essence of being
human.
- Discussing problems in business is often avoided,
stemming from a fear of criticism and the desire to maintain
a facade of optimism.

[PÁGINA 182]

Selling Through Problems
- Three genres of selling:
1.
Feature-Based Selling
: Focuses on product features without addressing customer
needs.
2.
Needs-Based Selling
: Claims to empathize with customer issues but often falls
back on features.
3.
Problem-Based Selling
: Effectively identifies and sells a problem, making the sale
compelling (e.g., Dollar Shave Club, Poo-Pourri).
The Role of Problems in Engagement
- Problems engage the mind and foster collaboration and
problem-solving among individuals.
- They inspire creativity and lead to innovative solutions by
prompting critical examination of challenges.
