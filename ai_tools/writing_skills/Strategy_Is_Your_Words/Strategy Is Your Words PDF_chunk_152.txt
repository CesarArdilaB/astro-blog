1.Question
Why do problems play such a central role in our lives,
both personally and within organizations?
Answer:Problems are intrinsic to the human
experience; they are the foundation of all stories we
tell and the catalyst for emotions and actions. At a
personal level, every person's internal dialogue is
filled with narratives about the challenges they face.
In business, problems give rise to companies, which
were originally created to help us navigate these
challenges. Ironically, while we crave problems for
the stories and solutions they offer, we
simultaneously fear them, leading to a dichotomy in
how we approach the notion of problems in
organizational contexts.
