The Mindset of Thinkers
Those who think for a living require minimal resources—air,
money, and a compelling topic. In contrast, thinkers who live
for their craft require community, practice, and mentorship,
but most importantly, they need a "gang" for support.
The Role of Mentors and Gangs
Mentors provide guidance and networking opportunities,
acting as metaphors for stability. However, a gang offers
stronger protection and collaborative support, fostering deep
intellectual connections among its members.
The Lone Wolf Concept
The idea of the “lone wolf” is misleading. Growth and
understanding come from partnerships and shared struggles.
Tethering oneself to like-minded strategists combats the
isolation that can accompany individual pursuits.
The Dynamics of Real Gangs
Gangs form under duress, providing mutual protection while

[PÁGINA 69]

grappling with internal conflicts. They utilize symbols and
rituals to establish identity and community.
Historical Examples
Gertrude Stein’s salon in Paris exemplified a creative gang,
nurturing artists like Picasso and Hemingway through
collaboration and artistic exchange. Similarly, the Wu-Tang
Clan showcases how a group can transcend individual
ambitions to create lasting art and influence.
The Call to Join a Gang
To thrive creatively, seek a vibrant group that fosters
collaboration and innovation. Embrace the tension and joy of
communal art-making. As ideas challenge societal norms, a
supportive gang is essential for protection and resilience.
Conclusion
In a world where individual pursuits may falter, the
collective strength of a gang empowers strategists to confront
challenges, create meaningful work, and find self-respect
amidst the noise of society.
