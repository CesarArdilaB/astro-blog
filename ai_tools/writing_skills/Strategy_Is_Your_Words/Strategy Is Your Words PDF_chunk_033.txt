Techniques for Developing Drama
To generate unexpected ideas and drama:
- Explore diverse environments and encourage teams to
connect observations with their projects.
- Pose absurd hypothetical scenarios to spark creativity.
- Embrace comedic techniques by considering the worst-case
scenarios for characters in discussions.
Presentation as Performance
Viewing presentations through the lens of directing can
instill drama and excitement. This involves crafting a
narrative, selecting participants, and creating engaging scenes
to deliver impactful outcomes.
Conclusion: The Role of Drama
In a world flooded with information, the strategist's challenge
is to reveal the underlying emotional narratives within data
clusters. Accept the dramatic aspects of your role—it's
essential for genuinely engaging communication. Embrace
drama while maintaining self-awareness to avoid negative
associations.

Critical Thinking
Key Point:The transformative power of drama in
strategic communication.
Critical Interpretation:Pollard suggests that drama
elevates presentations from mere information sharing to
emotionally engaging narratives, yet one should
critically assess whether this emphasis on emotional
connection can obscure the analytical rigor often
essential in strategic decision-making. Sources such as
"The Art of Strategy" by Avinash K. Dixit and Barry J.
Nalebuff indicate that while storytelling can enhance
persuasion, an overemphasis on emotional appeal risks
overshadowing critical logical analysis.

Chapter 21 Summary : Information Can
Crush You
Information in the Hands of Evil
In the wrong hands, information can be destructive, trying to
eliminate all uncertainty, and consequently stifling creativity
and independent thought. Information should inspire action
and understanding; however, when misused, it becomes a
tool for oppression, reducing complex concepts to mere
formulas.
The Danger of Data Worship
Data worship can turn information into a lifeless collection of
numbers, preventing meaningful interpretation and creativity.
This obsession with data leads to blind spots, highlighting a
human desire for control over a chaotic world. Information
should guide decision-making, but blind reliance on data can
lead to paralysis or misguided actions.
