3.Question
Why is breaking a pattern essential in effective
communication?

[PÁGINA 46]

Answer:Breaking a pattern is essential because humans are
conditioned to tune out repetitive stimuli, making them less
likely to pay attention. A surprising change is more likely to
engage their attention.
4.Question
What role does surprise play in keeping people's
attention?
Answer:Surprise jolts individuals into paying attention and
prompts them to think about the unexpected event,
encouraging them to seek reasons or explanations for it.
5.Question
How does the concept of 'schemas' relate to surprise in
the context of communication?
Answer:Schemas are mental models that help us predict what
will happen. When a surprising event violates these schemas,
it grabs our attention and forces us to reconsider our
understanding.
6.Question
Can you give an example of a surprising advertisement
that reinforces a core message?

[PÁGINA 47]

Answer:The Enclave minivan advertisement showed a family
driving, then unexpectedly depicted a crash, delivering a
powerful message about the importance of seatbelts. The
surprise reinforced the critical safety message.
7.Question
What does the author mean by 'gimmicky' surprise and
how can it be avoided?
Answer:'Gimmicky' surprise refers to shocking elements that
don't connect with the message and are merely distractions.
To avoid it, communicators should ensure surprises are
relevant and reinforce the core message.
8.Question
What is the 'Gap Theory' of curiosity and how does it
relate to attention?
Answer:The 'Gap Theory' posits that curiosity arises when
we recognize gaps in our knowledge. Highlighting these gaps
can maintain interest and compel audiences to seek answers.
9.Question
How does the Nordstrom example illustrate the power of
unexpected service?

[PÁGINA 48]

Answer:Nordstrom uses stories of employees providing
extraordinary service to break conventional customer service
schemas, thereby instilling a culture focused on exceptional
customer care.
10.Question
How can mysteries be effectively used in communication
to retain attention over extended periods?
Answer:Mysteries prompt audiences to engage by creating
the need for closure, encouraging them to stay invested in the
narrative until the mystery is solved.
11.Question
What shift in communication strategy is suggested to
make messages stickier?
Answer:Shifting from merely conveying information to
provoking curiosity and raising questions that the audience is
eager to answer is suggested to make messages stickier.
