About the book
In "Made to Stick," acclaimed authors Chip and Dan Heath
delve into the intriguing question of why some ideas resonate
while others fade away. Drawing on examples from urban
legends to impactful business concepts, they uncover six key
traits that contribute to the stickiness of messages. Through
engaging anecdotes and insightful principles—like the “human
scale principle,” the “Velcro Theory of Memory,” and
“curiosity gaps”—the Heath brothers provide a practical
framework for crafting memorable ideas. This enlightening
guide is not only a captivating exploration of successful
communication but also a transformative resource for anyone
looking to make their messages unforgettable. Whether you’re
a teacher, entrepreneur, or leader, "Made to Stick" equips you
with the tools to ensure your ideas leave a lasting impact.

About the author
Dan Heath and Chip Heath are renowned authors, speakers,
and thought leaders known for their expertise in
communication, psychology, and organizational behavior.
They gained widespread acclaim with their best-selling book,
"Made to Stick: Why Some Ideas Survive and Others Die,"
which explores the principles behind creating impactful and
memorable messages. Dan, a senior fellow at Duke
University's CASE Center, and Chip, a professor at Stanford
University, have collaborated on several influential works that
delve into the art of storytelling, decision-making, and
effective communication. Their unique blend of research and
real-world application has made their insights invaluable to
individuals and organizations seeking to inspire and engage
audiences.
