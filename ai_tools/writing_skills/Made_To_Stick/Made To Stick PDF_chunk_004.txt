Case Study: The Nature Conservancy
The Nature Conservancy struggled with protecting vast
environmentally critical areas in California. They shifted
from a concrete "bucks and acres" strategy (buying land) to
less tangible conservation methods, which risked donor
engagement. By redefining their goals to focus on preserving
"landscapes," they made strategies appear more concrete and
achievable, leading to better donor communication and
internal morale.
