Chapter 2 : Defining Your Customer as
the Hero - Shifting the Narrative Focus
In part 2 of Donald Miller’s "Building a StoryBrand," the
focus shifts to defining your customer as the hero of your
brand’s narrative. This strategic repositioning is paramount
because traditional marketing often makes the mistake of
casting the brand itself as the hero. Miller asserts that to truly
connect with an audience, a brand must instead position its
customers as the central protagonists of the story.
The importance of customer-centric storytelling cannot be
overstated. Customers are inundated with messaging daily,
and what cuts through the noise is a story where they see
themselves, their struggles, and their aspirations. By defining
the customer as the hero, you craft a message that resonates
on a deeply personal level, compelling them to engage with
your brand.
