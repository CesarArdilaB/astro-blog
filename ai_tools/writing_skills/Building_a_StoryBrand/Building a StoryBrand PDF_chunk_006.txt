Chapter 4 : Clarifying the Problem -
Highlighting Customer Pain Points
A pivotal element in the StoryBrand framework is the
clarification of the customer’s problem. It’s essential to
articulate these challenges effectively to establish a strong
connection with your audience. This step is not just about
mentioning that a problem exists; it’s about delving deep into
the nature of the problem your customers face, in a way that
resonates on multiple levels.
Understanding and defining the customer’s problem is
crucial because it sets the stage for the entire story.
According to Donald Miller, problems can be categorized
into three distinct types: external, internal, and philosophical.
The external problem is the visible, tangible issue that the
customer is facing. For example, if you’re selling a software
solution, the external problem might be that customers are
dealing with inefficient, time-consuming processes. External
problems are the hurdles that people are directly trying to
overcome and are often the first aspect of their pain points
that they’ll mention.

Internal problems, in many ways, are even more significant
because they get to the root of the issue - how the external
problems make the customer feel. These are the frustrations,
fears, and emotional hurdles. Using the same software
example, the internal problem might be the stress and
frustration that comes from dealing with outdated systems, or
the anxiety felt over whether these inefficiencies might affect
job performance or security. When your brand addresses
these internal issues, it demonstrates an understanding that
goes beyond surface-level disruptions.
Philosophical problems tap into a deeper sense of values and
beliefs. These are the issues related to the broader questions
of right and wrong, or good and bad. They reflect the
customer's desire for justice, integrity, and purpose. For
instance, that software solution is not just about efficiency
(external) or peace of mind (internal); it might also represent
a commitment to progress, innovation, or excellence
(philosophical). Highlighting these philosophical issues can
create a powerful emotional connection between the brand
and the customer.
